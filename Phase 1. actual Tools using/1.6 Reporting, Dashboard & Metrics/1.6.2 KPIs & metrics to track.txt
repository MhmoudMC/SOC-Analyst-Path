In soc, KPIs measure how well the team is performing. the plan highlights four main metrics to track:
*MTTD (Mean Time To Detect): The average time it takes for your tools or team to notice a cyber threat. Faster is better.
*MTTR (Mean Time To Respond): The average time it takes to contain and fix the threat after you detect it.
*Alert Volume: The total number of alarms the SOC receives every day or week. if this is to high, analysts get tired and might miss real attacks.
*False Positive Rate: The percentage of alarms that trigger on safe, normal activity. we tune our rules to keep this number as low as possible.

Since your plan requires building a Weekly SOC Metrics Dashboard, let's look at how all four work together:

1. MTTD & MTTR (The Speed Metrics)
There tell you how fast your "Detection" and "Response" are.
 * MTTD : The time from when an attacker start until your SIEM creates an alert. if this high, your detection rules might be too weak.
 * MTTR : the time from when the alert appears until you finish the "Containment" and "Resolution" Steps in your playbook, if this is high, your playbooks might be too confusing.

2.Alert Volume (The Capacity Metric)
This is simply the total number of "Notable Events" or alarms.
*Why it matters: If your Alert Volume goes up but your team size stays the same, your MTTR will naturally get worse because analysts are overwhelmed.

3.False Positive Rate (The Quality Metric)
A "False Positive" is an alert for something that isn't actually a threat {Like a developer running a test that looks like an attack).
*Tunning: Your plan requires "Alert noise reduction & tuning" if your False Positive rate is high (eg 80%) you are wasting your time on 8 out of 10 alerts.
